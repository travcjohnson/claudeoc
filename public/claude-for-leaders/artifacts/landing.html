Why Bluff Trail

Freshness isn't a slogan. It's a shipping policy.

No. 1

Roasted this week, not this quarter

Every bag is stamped with its roast date and ships within 48 hours. If a batch sits past seven days, it goes to the local food bank, not to you.

No. 2

Twelve kilos at a time

Small drums mean the roaster tastes every batch. When the marine layer changes the humidity, the roast curve changes with it. That attention is the whole product.

No. 3

Farmers we call by name

Three farms, three long-term contracts, prices set above Fair Trade floor. Ask us where your beans grew and we'll tell you the elevation.

Our Process

From green bean to your doorstep in four honest steps.

Step one

Source

Green beans from three partner farms in Ethiopia, Guatemala, and Sumatra — same families, every harvest.

Step two

Roast

Twelve-kilo batches on a 1987 Probat, profiled by hand and cupped against yesterday's roast before anything ships.

Step three

Rest

Every roast rests 24 hours to de-gas, then gets sealed with a one-way valve so it keeps developing, not fading.

Step four

Ship

Out the door within 48 hours of roast, with the roast date stamped where you can see it. Local orders ride a bike.
