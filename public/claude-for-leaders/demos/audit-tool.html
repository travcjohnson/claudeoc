Critical 3 issues

What we found

The lead-intake form at /contact posts without a CSRF token, and the site omits Content-Security-Policy and X-Frame-Options headers. The form endpoint also accepts unauthenticated repeat submissions with no rate limit.

Why it matters

This form is the primary way prospective clients reach the firm. Without these protections, an attacker can spoof submissions, spam the pipeline with junk leads, or embed the site in a fraudulent page. For an advisory firm, a spoofing incident is a trust problem, not just a technical one — and it can trigger compliance questions.

Recommended fix

Add CSRF tokens to the form handler, set a baseline security header policy (CSP, X-Frame-Options, HSTS), and rate-limit the endpoint. This is a half-day task for whoever maintains the site — no redesign required.

What we found

An 11.4 MB autoplay video loads before anything else on the homepage. Largest Contentful Paint on a mid-range phone is 4.8 seconds — nearly double Google's 2.5s threshold. 58% of the site's traffic is mobile.

Why it matters

Every extra second of load time costs roughly 7% of conversions. Visitors arriving from a referral or a LinkedIn post are making a first impression in that window — a slow homepage reads as a dated firm. Google also uses this metric in ranking, so it compounds the SEO findings below.

Recommended fix

Compress the video to under 2 MB (or replace with a poster image on mobile), lazy-load it below the first paint, and serve it from a CDN. Expected result: LCP under 2.5s, an immediate lift in mobile engagement.

What we found

A leftover noindex directive — likely from a staging deploy — is applied to all 14 pages under /services/. These pages describe the firm's core offerings and are invisible to Google.

Why it matters

These are the exact pages someone searching "wealth advisory Newport Beach" should land on. Right now that traffic goes to competitors. This is the single highest-leverage fix in the report: it is a one-line change that reopens an entire acquisition channel.

Recommended fix

Remove the noindex tag from the services template, resubmit the sitemap in Google Search Console, and verify indexing within 2 weeks. Effort: under one hour.

Warning 4 issues

What we found

26% of crawled pages (mostly insights articles and team bios) have no meta description; 9 share the identical generic title "Harborline Advisors".

Why it matters

The meta description is the ad copy Google shows under each result. Without it, Google improvises — often badly — and click-through rates drop 20–30% on affected pages. Thought-leadership articles are being published, then under-selling themselves in search.

Recommended fix

Generate descriptions for all 38 pages from existing page content (an afternoon with an AI-assisted workflow), then add a template rule so every new page requires one before publish.

What we found

Light-gray body text (#9B9B9B on white) measures 2.8:1 contrast — below the WCAG AA minimum of 4.5:1 — across the insights section and footer.

Why it matters

The firm's client base skews 50+, exactly the demographic most affected by low-contrast text. Beyond usability, accessibility gaps are an active litigation area — ADA website suits against professional-services firms have risen every year since 2020.

Recommended fix

Darken the body-text token to a compliant value (e.g. #5A5A5A or darker) in the site stylesheet. One CSS variable change fixes all 22 pages at once.

What we found

A third of the insights library predates 2025, including articles referencing outdated contribution limits and tax thresholds. Two of these rank on page one of Google and still receive meaningful traffic.

Why it matters

For an advisory firm, stale numbers are worse than no content — a prospect who spots an outdated tax figure questions everything else on the site. The two ranking articles are an opportunity: refreshed content on an already-ranking URL typically regains position within weeks.

Recommended fix

Refresh the two ranking articles first (update figures, add a "reviewed July 2026" line), then batch-review the remaining ten. Archive anything not worth updating with a redirect to a current piece.

What we found

Team headshots and office photography ship as 2–4 MB JPEGs at original camera resolution. Total image weight across the site: 61 MB. No WebP/AVIF variants, no responsive sizing.

Why it matters

The team page — the second most-visited page on the site — takes 6+ seconds to fully render on mobile. Prospects checking out the people they would be working with are hitting the slowest experience on the site.

Recommended fix

Run a one-time batch conversion to WebP with responsive sizes (a scripted task, ~2 hours), and configure the CMS to auto-convert future uploads. Expected image weight reduction: ~85%.

Passed 5 checks highlighted

What we found

Certificate is valid through March 2027, TLS 1.3 is enforced, and weak cipher suites are disabled. All traffic redirects cleanly to HTTPS.

Why it matters

Encryption in transit is table stakes for a financial firm, and this is done right. No action needed — keep the auto-renewal in place.

What we found

Every page is reachable within three clicks of the homepage, the sitemap is current, and there are no broken internal links or redirect chains among the 147 crawled pages.

Why it matters

Good information architecture is expensive to retrofit — this foundation means the SEO fixes above will take effect quickly once the noindex issue is resolved.

What we found

No horizontal scrolling, tap targets meet minimum size guidance, and text remains readable without zooming on all tested devices. The layout itself is not the mobile problem — the media weight is.

Why it matters

Once the video and image fixes land, mobile users get a genuinely good experience with no additional design work.

What we found

Every services page ends with a consistent "Schedule a conversation" call-to-action, and the booking flow works end-to-end in under 60 seconds.

Why it matters

The conversion path is already well designed. The findings above are about getting more qualified visitors into this path, not rebuilding it.

What we found

Navigation, forms, and the booking flow are all operable by keyboard alone, with visible focus indicators throughout. Skip-to-content link present and functional.

Why it matters

Keyboard access is the hardest accessibility fix to retrofit, and it is already in place. Fixing the contrast issue closes most of the remaining WCAG AA gap.
